sun flowers

The sunflower is a tall, bright yellow flower known for its large, round face and ability to track the sun throughout the day. It's admired for its vibrant appearance and is often associated with positivity and warmth. Sunflowers are also cultivated for their seeds, which are used in cooking and as snacks.

Common Sunflower

🌻 How to Care for Sunflowers

☀️ Sunlight: Sunflowers need at least 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily. Place them in a south-facing spot for best results.

💧 Watering: Water deeply but infrequently. Keep the soil moist but not waterlogged. Young sunflowers need more frequent watering until established.

🌱 Soil: Use well-drained, nutrient-rich soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5. Sunflowers are not very picky but thrive in loose, fertile ground.

🌡️ Temperature: Sunflowers prefer warm temperatures between 70–78°F (21–26°C). They are sensitive to frost, so plant after the last frost date.

✂️ Pruning: Remove dead or fading flower heads to encourage new blooms. Cut stems at a 45-degree angle if using as cut flowers.

🧪 Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer once a month during the growing season. Avoid over-fertilizing as it can reduce flowering.
